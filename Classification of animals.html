The Classification of Animals


Animals are a major group of organisms, classified as the kingdom Animalia or Metazoa. In general they are multicellular, capable of locomotion and responsive to their environment, and feed by consuming other organisms. Animals have several characteristics that set them apart from other living things.

Common Characteristics of Animals

Animals live all around us. Here are some common characteristics of animals:

  • They cannot make their own food and are hence heterotrophs
  • They have special organs for locomotion
  • They have limited growth unlike plants
  • They are mulicellular
  • They do not have a cell wall
  • They contain a cell membrane and membrane-bound cell organelles
  • They have a well developed nervous system
  • They are eukaryotic as they have a well defined nucleus

Difference between Vertebrates and Invertebrates

Animals are divided into two categories on the basis if a backbone is present. If a backbone is present, the animal is a vertebrate. If a backbone is not present, the animal is an invertebrate. The table given below lists all the differences between vertebrates and invertebrates.


Vertebrates Invertebrates
They have an internal skeleton They do not have any internal skeleton
Heart is on the ventral side of the body Heart, if present, is on the dorsal side of the body
Nerve cord is hollow and is present on the dorsal side Nerve cord is solid and is present on the ventral side
They have two pairs of limbs They have three or more pairs of limbs
